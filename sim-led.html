What are you seeing?

In a InGaN quantum well — the light-emitting layer in modern LEDs — the indium (In) concentration varies randomly at the nanoscale. This spatial disorder creates a random potential V(x,y) for electrons and holes.

Rather than solving for every quantum state, the localization landscape W = 1/u gives an effective potential that immediately reveals where carriers accumulate. The carrier density ρ ∝ exp(−W/kT) is a Boltzmann-weighted occupation of the landscape wells.

This predicts the Stokes shift (emission redshifted from absorption), the internal quantum efficiency droop at high current, and the spatial distribution of recombination — all without expensive full quantum calculations. See: Banon, Pelletier, Weisbuch, Filoche & Mayboroda, Phys. Rev. B 2022.
