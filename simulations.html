Interactive Simulations

Browser-based simulations illustrating the mathematics of wave localization, Anderson localization, and related phenomena from Svitlana Mayboroda's research. Each runs entirely in your browser — no installation required.

Localization Peaks

A 3D view of the localization landscape u(x,y) — the peaks are the quantum wells where states concentrate. Red lines on the floor show the watershed partition, dividing the domain into one region per peak. Inspired by Marcel Filoche’s original visualization.

Interactive · 3D view · Watershed

Wave Localization Explorer

Explore the localization landscape W = 1/u for the operator −Δ + V, watch wave pulses scatter and trap in a disordered medium, and visualize the disorder potential itself.

Interactive · 2D

Eigenfunction vs. Landscape

Compare low-energy eigenfunctions of −Δ + V (computed via power iteration) side-by-side with the landscape prediction W = 1/u. The landscape pinpoints localization regions without solving the eigenvalue problem.

Interactive · 2D · Eigensolve

Mobility Edge & Percolation

Drag an energy threshold and watch the landscape sublevel set grow from isolated pockets into a percolating cluster crossing the domain — a visual picture of the Anderson mobility edge transition.

Interactive · 2D · Percolation

LED / Semiconductor Quantum Well

Simulate a disordered InGaN-like alloy and see where electrons and holes concentrate in the localization landscape. A temperature slider controls the carrier density distribution.

Interactive · 2D · Semiconductor

Cantor Set Construction

Watch the Cantor set emerge through iterated removal, with a live fractal dimension readout. Extend to 2D Cantor dust, or adjust the removal fraction. Connected to the theory of sets with lower-dimensional boundaries.

Animated · Fractal · 1D / 2D
