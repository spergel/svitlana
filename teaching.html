  • Laura Shou — University of Minnesota Fall 2022–2023
  • Tomas Merchan Rodriguez — University of Minnesota, Fall 2020–2022 PhD from Kent State University (B. Jaye & F. Nazarov). Worked on regularity of solutions and the Robin BVP for elliptic operators on domains with low-dimensional boundaries. Assistant Professor at Clayton State University as of 2022.
  • Shiwen Zhang — University of Minnesota, Fall 2019–August 2022 PhD from UC Irvine (S. Jitomirskaya, 2016); postdoc at Michigan State (A. Volberg). Worked on localization landscape theory, landscape law for eigenvalue approximation, and Poisson-landscape equations. Assistant Professor at UMass Lowell as of Fall 2022.
  • Linhan Li — University of Minnesota, Fall 2019–July 2022 PhD from Brown University (J. Pipher). Dunham Jackson Assistant Professor. Worked on properties of the Green function and elliptic measures for operators with nonsmooth coefficients. Lecturer at the University of Edinburgh as of Fall 2022.
  • Guillermo Rey — University of Minnesota, Fall 2018–Spring 2019 PhD from Michigan State University, 2015. Research focused on localization of eigenfunctions and harmonic measure on rough boundaries; previously a software engineer at Google.
  • Robert Viator — University of Minnesota / IMA, 2016–2017 PhD from Louisiana State University (R. Lipton). Worked on the localization landscape approach to Weyl law in periodic structures.
  • Simon Bortz — University of Minnesota, September 2016–2018 PhD from University of Missouri (S. Hofmann, Spring 2016). Worked on Dirichlet, Neumann, and regularity problems in higher co-dimension, and layer potentials for the generalized Schrödinger operator. Acting Assistant Professor at University of Washington as of Fall 2018.
  • Joseph Feneuil — University of Minnesota, September 2015–Spring 2018 PhD from University of Grenoble (E. Russ). Dunham Jackson postdoctoral researcher. Worked on analogues of harmonic measure in higher co-dimension. Research Assistant Professor at Temple University as of Fall 2018.
  • Stephen Lewis — University of Minnesota, September 2014–January 2015 PhD from University of Washington (T. Toro, 2014). Worked on localization of waves; left to work in industry in 2015.
  • Blair Davey — University of Minnesota, 2013–2015 PhD from University of Chicago (C. Kenig). Worked on boundary value problems for Schrödinger-type elliptic operators. Assistant Professor at CUNY as of Fall 2018.
  • Ariel Barton — Purdue University and University of Minnesota, 2010–2013 PhD from University of Chicago (C. Kenig). Worked on higher order elliptic problems on Lipschitz domains. Produced 5 research papers, 3 joint with S. Mayboroda, and 1 monograph accepted to Memoirs of the AMS. Assistant Professor at University of Arkansas, Fayetteville as of Fall 2018.

  • Cole Jeznach — ETH Zurich Co-supervised with Max Engelstein. Working on the study of elliptic measure outside of sets with low dimension.

  • Eli Johnson — University of Minnesota Worked on boundary regularity for the fractional Laplacian. Currently a graduate student at the University of Washington.

  • Yaowen Gu — University of Minnesota, 2012 Senior project on harmonic measure and Brownian motion.

  • Joseph Pate — University of Minnesota, 2016–2017 Worked on properties of the landscape function for the Neumann problem and localization of Neumann eigenfunctions. UROP award recipient; supported in part by the REU project within S. Mayboroda’s CAREER grant.
