What are you seeing?

The left panel shows a quantum eigenfunction φn — a solution to the Schrödinger equation (−Δ + V)φ = Eφ in a disordered potential V, computed here via power iteration with deflation. Each eigenfunction is localized: it concentrates in a small region and decays elsewhere.

The right panel shows the localization landscape W = 1/u, where u solves (−Δ + V)u = 1. Discovered by Filoche & Mayboroda (2012), W predicts — without computing any eigenfunction — exactly which regions will trap quantum states. Dark valleys in W are the predicted localization wells; compare them with the bright peaks of each eigenfunction.

Use the eigenmode slider to step through the first six modes (computed from lowest to highest energy). Each one sits in a valley of W. See: Filoche & Mayboroda, PNAS 2012.
