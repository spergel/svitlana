What are you seeing?

In a disordered medium (like a semiconductor with impurities, or a room with irregular walls), waves do not spread freely — they get trapped in certain regions and blocked from others. This phenomenon is called Anderson localization.

Svitlana Mayboroda and collaborators discovered the localization landscape: a single function — solution to −Δu + Vu = 1 — whose reciprocal W = 1/u acts as an “effective potential” predicting exactly where waves will localize, without ever solving the full eigenvalue problem.

In Landscape mode, bright regions are barriers; dark valleys are where quantum states concentrate. In Wave mode, click anywhere to create a pulse and watch it scatter and trap in the disordered landscape. The long-time wave pattern closely matches the landscape prediction.
