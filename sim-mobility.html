What are you seeing?

The localization landscape W = 1/u acts as an effective potential. Quantum states with energy E are confined to regions where W(x,y) < E — the sublevel set highlighted in this simulation.

At low energies, this set consists of isolated pockets: states are localized. As E increases, the pockets grow and eventually merge into a path that crosses the entire domain. This is the Anderson mobility edge — the critical energy at which the system transitions from localized to extended (delocalized) states.

Mayboroda and collaborators proved that this transition is precisely a percolation transition in the sublevel sets of W. See: Filoche, Mayboroda, Pelletier & Delande, 2023.
